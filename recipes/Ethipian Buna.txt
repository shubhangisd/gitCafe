Ethiopian Buna (Coffee)

Ingredients:

1/2 cup whole coffee beans (preferably Ethiopian coffee beans)
2 cups water
Optional: sugar, ground cardamom, or other spices to taste
Instructions:

Roast the Coffee Beans:

Place the coffee beans in a dry skillet over medium heat.
Stir frequently to ensure even roasting and prevent burning.
Roast until the beans are dark brown and emit a rich, aromatic fragrance (about 5-7 minutes).
Grind the Coffee Beans:

Allow the roasted beans to cool for a few minutes.
Grind the beans to a medium-coarse consistency using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
Prepare the Coffee:

Boil 2 cups of water in a kettle or saucepan.
Add the ground coffee to a traditional Ethiopian coffee pot (Jebena) or a regular saucepan.
Pour the boiling water over the coffee grounds.
Brew the Coffee:

If using a Jebena, place it over low heat and let the coffee simmer for 10-15 minutes. Avoid boiling the coffee vigorously; a gentle simmer is preferred.
If using a regular saucepan, reduce heat to low and let the coffee steep for 10-15 minutes.
Serve the Coffee:

Pour the brewed coffee into small cups.
Optional: Add sugar or ground cardamom to taste. You can also add a pinch of other spices if desired.
Enjoy:

Ethiopian Buna is often enjoyed in a ceremonial context, sometimes accompanied by a small snack or in a social setting with family and friends.
